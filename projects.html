Featured Projects

  • Tacteyele
    PennApps XV 3rd Place
    Tacteyele is a hands-free way to use your computer. Originally developed at PennApps XV, it was inspired by one of our friends who has an uncle with Parkinson's. Users tilt their head to move the mouse, wink to click, and speak to type or do keyboard shortcuts. Tacteyele runs on Python 2.7, OpenCV, and Watson SPT.

    You can peruse the code, watch the demo, or see the project submission.
  • PebbleTennis
    HackRU Fall 2015 2nd Place, Best Hardware Hack, Best Use of SendGrid, Best Use of Pebble
    PebbleTennis is a smart tennis racket that uses accelerometer data to track your strokes and sends it to a server, which emails the data to you and your coach. At HackRU, my friend and I (who both play tennis) attached a Pebble smartwatch onto a tennis racket and used the Pebble's accelerometer.
    PebbleTennis runs on Node.js and Pebble.js

    You can peruse the code, watch the demo, or see the project submission.
